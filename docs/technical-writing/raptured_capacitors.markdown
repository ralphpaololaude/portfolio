# Component failure due to ruptured capacitors

{: .warning }
Follow *Control of Hazardous Energy* (COHE) work instructions. This includes applicable lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures.

## Problem

Damaged or ruptured capacitors can leak oil and spread carbon particles.

Oil that has leaked from the body of the capacitor can dry out and collect dirt. These debris can deposited on components near the capacitors, causing component failure.

## Symptoms

- Leaked oil and carbon debris collect around the area of the capacitor.
- Components around the capacitor have failed due to debris caused by the ruptured capacitor.

## Action

When replacing a ruptured capacitor, also check for leaked oil and debris on nearby components, especially on circuit boards.

If contaminants are found, do a thorough check and cleaning of adjacent components.

If you see circuit boards remain unrecoverable after cleaning, replace the board to prevent future issues.
